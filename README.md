# My First Step Into Cybersecurity – Understanding Phishing Attacks

When I first started learning cybersecurity, one of the most common terms I heard everywhere was “phishing.” At first, I thought it was some highly technical hacking method, but after learning about it, I realized phishing attacks are actually very simple and dangerous at the same time.

In this blog, I’ll share what I understood about phishing attacks in a beginner-friendly way.



## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers try to trick people into revealing sensitive information such as:

* Passwords
* Bank details
* OTPs
* Personal information

Usually, attackers pretend to be trusted companies or people.

For example:
You may receive an email saying:

> “Your bank account will be blocked. Click here to verify your account.”

Many people panic and click the link without checking whether it is real or fake.

That is how phishing works.

---

## How Phishing Attacks Work

A phishing attack usually follows these steps:

1. The attacker creates a fake email, message, or website.
2. The victim receives the message.
3. The victim clicks the malicious link.
4. The victim enters sensitive information.
5. The attacker steals the data.

Sometimes phishing links even install malware on the victim’s system.

---

## Common Types of Phishing

### 1. Email Phishing

Fake emails pretending to be from companies like banks, Amazon, or Netflix.

### 2. SMS Phishing (Smishing)

Fake messages sent through SMS.

Example:

> “Your package delivery failed. Click here.”

### 3. Voice Phishing (Vishing)

Attackers call victims pretending to be customer support or bank officials.

### 4. Spear Phishing

A targeted phishing attack aimed at a specific person or organization.



## Signs of a Phishing Email

While practicing online labs, I noticed some common signs in phishing emails:

* Spelling mistakes
* Urgent language
* Suspicious links
* Unknown sender address
* Requests for passwords or OTPs

For example:
A real company usually will not ask for your password through email.



## How to Stay Safe

Here are some basic safety tips I learned:

* Never click unknown links
* Check email addresses carefully
* Enable two-factor authentication
* Use strong passwords
* Keep software updated
* Verify suspicious messages before responding

Even simple awareness can stop many attacks.



## What I Learned

Before learning cybersecurity, I used to think hacking only involved advanced coding and complicated tools. But phishing showed me that many attacks actually target human mistakes instead of systems.

This made me realize that cybersecurity is not only about technology but also about awareness and smart decision-making.



## Conclusion

Phishing is one of the most common cyber attacks today because it is simple and effective. Understanding how phishing works is an important first step for anyone starting in cybersecurity.

As a beginner, learning these basics helped me become more careful online and more interested in cybersecurity.

I’m still learning more every day, and this is one of my first steps into the cybersecurity field.
